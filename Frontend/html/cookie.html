1. What Is the session_token Cookie?

The session_token cookie is a first-party cookie used to maintain your session while you use our website. It contains information necessary for authentication and session management, such as:

  • User ID
  • Session token
  • Username
  • Group codes (if applicable)

This data is stored in a JSON format and may include a session expiration timestamp, depending on how the session is configured.

2. Purpose of the Cookie

We use the session_token cookie strictly for the following purposes:

  • Authentication: To verify your identity after login.
  • Session Management: To keep you logged in across pages.
  • Security: To prevent unauthorized access to your account.
  • User Experience: To enable features that rely on knowing your login status.

3. Cookie Type and Duration

  • Type: First-party, strictly necessary cookie
  • Expires: The cookie is set to expire automatically after a predefined time (e.g., when the session ends or after 1 hour of inactivity). This expiration may vary depending on the platform‚Äôs settings.

4. Do We Share This Cookie with Third Parties?

No. The session_token cookie is used only by our website and is not shared with any third-party services or advertisers.

5. Can You Disable This Cookie?

Because the session_token cookie is essential for logging in and maintaining your session, it cannot be disabled without disrupting core functionality. If you choose to block or delete this cookie, you may be logged out or unable to access secure parts of the website.

6. Data Security and Privacy

All data stored in the cookie is encoded and handled in accordance with our Privacy Policy. Sensitive information such as passwords is never stored in cookies.
